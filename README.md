# 🤖 User Trust in AI-Based Decision Support Systems
## Investigating the Impact of Explanation Modality (Visual vs. Textual) and Risk Levels on User Trust

---

### 📋 Project Overview
[cite_start]This academic research explores the **"Black Box" paradox** in Artificial Intelligence[cite: 15, 255]. [cite_start]As AI systems become more complex, the gap in human interpretability leads to **"Algorithm Aversion,"** threatening technology adoption in critical fields[cite: 16, 256]. [cite_start]This study empirically examines how the modality of explanation (Visual vs. Textual) and varying levels of risk affect user trust and the willingness to rely on the system. [cite: 18, 57, 258, 293]

---

### 📊 Key Research Findings

* [cite_start]**🏆 The Visual Advantage**: Visual explanations achieved significantly higher perceived trust scores (**5.4**) compared to textual ones (**4.1**) on a 7-point scale. [cite: 21, 173, 261, 408]
* [cite_start]**🛡️ Resilience in High-Risk Situations**: In high-risk scenarios, reliance on textual explanations plummeted to **31%**, while visual explanations maintained a high reliance level of **73%**. [cite: 22, 179, 262, 414]
* [cite_start]**⚓ Cognitive Anchoring**: Visualization serves as a **"cognitive anchor,"** enabling users to verify machine decisions quickly and bridging the trust gap in extreme situations. [cite: 24, 264]

---

### 🔬 Methodology

* [cite_start]**⚙️ Design**: Controlled quantitative experiment (**N=128**) using a 2x2 factorial design. [cite: 20, 127, 260, 362]
* [cite_start]**🏥 Context**: Medical decision-making scenarios. [cite: 20, 125, 260, 360]
* [cite_start]**⚖️ Regulation**: Conducted against the backdrop of the **EU AI Act of 2024**, which establishes the legal "Right to Explanation". [cite: 17, 46, 73, 257, 308]
