User Trust in AI-Based Decision Support Systems: The Impact of Modality and Risk
Project Overview
This academic research explores the "Black Box" paradox in Artificial Intelligence. As AI systems become more complex, the gap in human interpretability leads to "Algorithm Aversion," threatening technology adoption in critical fields. This study empirically examines how explanation modality (Visual vs. Textual) affects user trust and willingness to rely on AI, mediated by risk levels.

Key Research Findings

The Visual Advantage: Visual explanations achieved significantly higher perceived trust scores (5.4) compared to textual ones (4.1).
+3


Resilience in High-Risk Situations: In high-risk scenarios, reliance on textual explanations plummeted to 31%, while visual explanations maintained a high reliance level of 73%.
+3


Cognitive Anchoring: Visualization serves as a "cognitive anchor," enabling users to verify machine decisions quickly and bridging the trust gap in extreme situations.

Methodology

Design: Controlled quantitative experiment (N=128) using a 2x2 factorial design.
+3


Context: Medical decision-making scenarios.
+3


Regulation: Conducted against the backdrop of the EU AI Act of 2024, which establishes the "Right to Explanation".
